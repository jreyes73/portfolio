About Me

Hi! I'm Julian. First of all, I really appreciate you visiting my site so I hope I am able to provide with some useful information. I have spent the last 11 years living in Seoul, South Korea; originally as an English teacher and most recently as a content developer for an education company. It was during my time as a content developer that I became enthralled by creating content and projects digitally. As a content developer, I created interactive lessons, designed curriculums, authored textbooks for young learners. While creating educational content was an experience that I will cherish for a long time, I knew that I still wasn't reaching my highest potential.

After briefly exploring graphic design, I discovered software development. A couple of years ago, I would have never imagined pursuing a career as software developer, since I had only really knew how to use the most common programs (Chrome, Word, PowerPoint, - I had only recently learned the Adobe programs) After all this, is coming from a guy that wanted to create his own digital board but could only make a prototype on PowerPoint since I hadn't had a clue of how to write a single line of code. I have now been on my coding journey since summer 2023, and I have enjoyed every second (even the frustrating times) of learning through Codecademy's Full-Stack Engineering career path.

With each day of learning, my confidence is growing. I am looking to complete the program in the next few months and as I do that I have been working on side projects to further solidify my new skills. Some of those projects include this portfolio website, a template resume website (to potentially offer as a service), and my board game idea. I am looking for as much experience and practice as possible so that I am eventually able to fully make a career switch to either a front-end developer or maybe a full-stack developer. I know that with regular practice and dedication, I will reach my goal and all the late nights studying will eventually pay off.

With that being said, I hope that this website will showcase my current skills and my passion for software development. While I will acknowledge that I have a long way to go, I also see that I have come a long way. And for those that are considering a career in software development or even making a career switch, I would like to refer you to the quote by Greek philosopher, Epictetus - "First say to yourself what you would be; and then do what you have to do." It's a quote that I find rather relatable, because while I didn't know a thing about coding before I started my journey, I did know that I could reach my goal if I set my mind to it. So please, feel free to reach out to me if you have any questions or inquries.
